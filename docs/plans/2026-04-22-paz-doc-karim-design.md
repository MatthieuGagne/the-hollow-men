# Character Design: Paz (Bard) & Doc Karim (White Mage)

**Session date:** 2026-04-22

---

## Paz — Bard

**Class:** Bard
**Bureau status:** Uncategorized — Bureau has no file. Ley terminals register something; the assessment protocol doesn't know what to do with it.

### Background
Grew up in the Meridian — wrong geometry from birth, Bureau cordon always porous around her. Now lives and performs in the Sprawl. Anti-establishment against the whole system: Bureau, megacorps, WARDEN. Not a political position she arrived at — a conclusion she reached at twelve years old watching her community get processed.

### Core Wound — The Regularization
When Paz was young, the Bureau ran an administrative sweep through the Meridian — not a crackdown, something worse. They processed it. Assigned ley terminal reference numbers to buildings, families, people. Anyone with detectable output got assessed. Several didn't come back. No arrests, no scenes. Just forms, filed correctly. The bureaucracy didn't hate the Meridian. It *administrated* it. Paz watched people she grew up with become output readings in a system that didn't know their names. The anger isn't personal. It's structural. It's been there since she was twelve.

### Psychology
**Coping:** Performing, fighting, looking outward. She suspects her own power does something beyond ordinary performance — she doesn't dwell on it. There's work to do.
**Won't examine:** That she moves people without their consent — ally and enemy simultaneously — the same way the Bureau harvests output without consent. She tells herself it's different because her cause is right. That's the Bureau's logic too.

TBD — she follows a thread from the Regularization (where did those people go, what was their output used for) that leads to the same place the Vesper case leads. She doesn't join Reid. She arrives independently.

### Appearance
Latina, warm brown skin. Long bright orange braids — loose, they move when she performs. Dark expressive eyes. A battered long leather jacket, dark and Sprawl-worn on the outside, vivid red lining when it opens. Dark fitted underlayers, worn boots. A heavily modified electric guitar — the body predates her, salvaged or passed down; the modifications are hers. The instrument is present, not displayed. Dominant color: **burnt orange / deep red**.

### Relationships
- **Iris**: Iris has clocked Paz from operating in the same Sprawl underground. Paz doesn't know Iris exists. The asymmetry is the dynamic.
- **Everyone else**: TBD

### Mechanics
Abilities affect the whole battlefield simultaneously — ally buffs and enemy debuffs at once. The Bureau never thought to meter performance as a psychic channel. No Grade on file; assessment protocol returns an error before a number.

## Doc Karim — White Mage

**Class:** White Mage
**Bureau status:** Unlicensed — tolerated. The Sprawl needs healers. The Bureau knows he exists and has chosen not to act. That arrangement ends the moment he associates with Reid's group. He knows this. He joins anyway.

**Handle:** Doc. Everyone calls him this. Karim is his actual name — on no file anywhere.

### Background
Sprawl-born, Sprawl-stayed. South Asian heritage (Pakistani or Bengali). Mid-50s, unlicensed community healer. He's been patching up Sprawl residents for decades — megacorp workers with industrial injuries, people who can't afford Bureau-licensed medical care, practitioners who've been calibrated and lost more than just their output. No Academy, no license, no Grade on file. The Bureau tolerates him because the Sprawl needs healers and he stays in his lane. He's never stayed in his lane. He's just never been worth the paperwork.

### Core Wound / Psychology
TBD — deliberately open for story design. His psychology will be fleshed out in narrative context.

What is known: he joins the investigation knowing exactly what it costs — his Bureau tolerance, his ability to operate in the Sprawl, the arrangement that's kept him safe for decades. He sees something in a patient's wounds that has no medical category, and he needs to understand what's doing this to people. That need is stronger than the cost. That's who he is before we know his wound.

### Investigation Entry
Someone from Reid's group arrives badly hurt. He treats them, asks no questions — the way he always does. But what he sees during treatment is entity-adjacent: injuries that follow no pattern he recognizes, something wrong at the tissue level that his decades of Sprawl medicine have no category for. He needs to understand it. He's in.

### Appearance
South Asian heritage, warm medium-dark skin. Mid-50s, stocky build — the kind of man who looks like he's been carrying things for a long time. Dark hair going fully grey, worn loose and slightly unkempt — not regulation, not styled, just not prioritized. Dark warm eyes. A worn dark green utility jacket, heavily pocketed, patched in places. Medical supplies visible on his person. He looks like what he is: someone who works with his hands in difficult conditions for decades. Dominant color: **deep worn green**.

### Relationships
- Stranger to everyone in the party at entry
- No prior relationships to navigate — the group forms a read on him fresh
