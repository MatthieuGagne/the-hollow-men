# Entity-Touch Visual Language

## The Distinction

Wrong geometry belongs to space, not bodies. The Meridian's architecture distorts because space
is distorting. People distort differently — they *drain*. The entity is a consumer, not a sculptor.

Wrong geometry (impossible angles, impossible light, non-Euclidean space) applies to:
- The Meridian's architecture, escalating as the party descends
- Environmental phenomena at the wound and in the deep geometry zones

Entity-touch visual language applies to characters. One consistent register for what the entity
does to people.

## Entity-Touch Visual Language

Characters touched by the entity show:

- **Pallor going greenish** — the color draining, the self receding
- **Moments of near-transparency** — the person becoming less present in the world
- **Glow in the eyes** — the entity looking *through* a person still partially there
- **Forgetting** — loss of continuity, small at first, larger as the touch deepens

## Severity Gradient

| Stage | Who | Eyes | Body |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early | The kid | Faint glow — attributable to reflections if you aren't looking | Pallor barely noticeable, faint greenish cast starting |
| Mid | Karim's patients | Visible glow, undeniable | Clear greenish pallor, transparency moments |
| Advanced | Vesper | Mostly glow; the entity occupies the driver's seat intermittently | Near-transparent at the edges, pallor deep |
| Shade (Tier 1 enemy) | Fully consumed | Absent — not dark, just gone | Wrong-green edge bleed on the silhouette |
| Vessel (Tier 2 enemy) | Entity actively piloting the body | Full wrong-green — entity looking out, not through | Body distorting; the entity doesn't understand anatomy |

### The Logic of the Eye States

The glow in the eyes is the entity looking *through* a person who is still partially there. When
the person is fully consumed and becomes a Shade, there is no longer a person for the entity to
look through — the glow goes out. The eyes become absent.

The Vessel's full wrong-green eyes are different: the entity has moved back in and is actively
piloting the body. It is looking *out*, not through. Shade = unoccupied hollow. Vessel = entity
wearing the body again.

## Kos — A Different Contact

Kos is not on the consumption gradient. Something came *through* him during a failed Output
Calibration — not consuming him, coming the other way. The visual result:

- Severely pale ("cold, almost bloodless, ice in it rather than fragility" — already in his description)
- Eye color distinct from wrong-green (**TBD** — not consumption, not Bureau blue; something from
  the between-space coming through)
- Eyes that track things nobody else can see

Kos does not show the greenish pallor, near-transparency, or forgetting of consumed characters.
His pallor is different in quality — cold and ice-like rather than draining. His condition is
categorically different from consumption.

## Pixel Art Constraints

At 16×16px character sprite scale:

- Color shift (pallor → greenish cast) is readable
- A glow in the eyes is readable as a color accent point
- Near-transparency can be expressed through lighter pixel values at sprite edges
- Wrong geometry on character bodies is **not readable** — impossible angles and non-Euclidean
  distortion don't survive at this scale

Wrong geometry is rendered at the tileset/environment level only.

## The Kid — What Changed

The kid's original "wrong geometry tells" (shadow that doesn't match, reflection slightly out of
sync) are replaced throughout all design documents. The kid is entity-touched — not a resonance
source with environmental side effects, but a person being slowly consumed, early stage. Their
unmetered output resonates with the between-space as the entity draws at them, which worsens the
surrounding geometry as a secondary effect. The geometry worsening is real; its cause is the
entity-touch, not the unregistered output on its own.

## Vesper — What Changed

Vesper's "shadow doesn't match" language is removed throughout. Her uncanny quality is rendered
through entity-touch: greenish pallor, near-transparency at the edges, wrong-green glow in the
eyes. The wrong geometry bending *around* her at the wound is retained — that is environmental
space reacting to her presence, not a body-geometry tell.
