# Case 3: The Wound (working title)

## Overview

The party descends into the deeper Meridian following the direction of the anomaly Iris identified in Case 2. Wrong geometry escalates across four zones. Kos — a Bureau escapee hiding in the deep geometry, entity-touched from a failed Output Calibration — is encountered in Zone 3 and does not join. The dungeon ends at a ley architecture wound: Bureau conduit lines running through the between-space, the harvest mechanism physically exposed. Margot's document case data closes here. Vesper is glimpsed at the far edge of the wound. The party returns to the hub changed.

## Beat Map

### BEAT 1 — THE HUB: SETTLING IN (Iris's apartment)

The party has arrived. Character positions are established through exploration and first-impression dialogue: Margot at the kitchen table, document case open. Karim in his corner — he has opinions about the lighting. Iris in the workshop, door open now. Reid with no fixed spot.

The kid's wrong geometry tells are present. A shadow that doesn't match. A reflection slightly out of sync. Iris manages this matter-of-factly — she's been doing it alone for eighteen months. Nothing in her manner suggests it's alarming. It is alarming.

*[Hub — player has full control. Rest/Save and Party Management active. First hub exploration and first-impression dialogue.]*

### BEAT 2 — THE SYNTHESIS (Iris's apartment — kitchen table)

Margot, Karim, and Iris cross-reference at the kitchen table. The three data sets converged on the Meridian pocket in Case 2 — but the pocket was the kid's resonance responding to something deeper, not generating it. Vesper's logs, Margot's records, Karim's files all pointed to a symptom. The source is further in.

The key discovery: Karim notices the kid's wrong geometry tells have been worsening since the party arrived. More unmetered practitioners in the apartment, more output in the space, stronger resonance. The kid is a compass needle — pointing at the source, not being it.

*[Investigation mode at the hub — scripted dialogue with light player interaction. Player selects topics (Vesper's logs, Margot's records, Karim's files, the kid's tells). The compass needle discovery is the payoff beat once all topics are exhausted.]*

### BEAT 3 — THE TRIGGER (Iris's apartment)

The kid's geometry spikes — worst it's been. Something deeper in the Meridian is pulling harder. Iris reads it immediately: it's been building since the party arrived. It won't stop building.

They can't wait.

### ZONE 2 — THE ARCHITECTURE (Meridian — intermediate depth)

Streets that don't connect as they should. Mid-tier encounters. Karim reads the building material: entity-contact scarring in the architecture itself — structures that have been ley-exposed long enough that the damage is load-bearing. He has the category. Having it is worse than not having one.

At the edge of the geometry: movement. Something that knows these spaces better than the party. Not hostile. Not communicating. Gone before the party can close.

### ZONE 3 — THE DEEP MERIDIAN (Meridian — deep)

Wrong geometry is aggressive. Rooms have rotated. Corridors loop. Loot caches — left by people who made it this far and didn't come back. High-tier encounters.

**KOS ENCOUNTER:** The party rounds a corner and Kos is there. Not hiding — he knew they were coming. Thin, pale, carrying something he can't name. He's been in the deep geometry long enough that it doesn't bother him. For him, it's the only place in the city where something inside him is quiet.

He moves first — Bureau escapee, everyone who finds him is a threat. A brief forced fight. Iris talks through it before anyone is badly hurt: she reads him immediately (not Bureau, not enforcement, running from the same machine). He survives things he shouldn't. The entity-touch is visible in how he moves through the wrong geometry. The party notices. Nobody says anything yet.

What follows: wariness, not warmth. He knows what's at the end of the direction. He won't say what it is — not because he's cagey, but because he doesn't have language for it. Felt knowledge, not theoretical. He gives them enough:

*"There's a place where the walls go wrong in a specific way. You'll know it. Don't touch the architecture."*

No explanation for the warning. He disappears back into the geometry before the party can press him.

Iris, in motion, one beat after he's gone — she says something to Reid. Brief. Not a speech. Something that makes clear she knows exactly what she's walking into now and chose it this time. Then she keeps moving.

*[ATB combat: forced encounter with Kos — health threshold or turn count triggers Iris interruption, not a win/loss state. High-tier entity-adjacent encounters. Kos does not join.]*

### ZONE 4 — THE WOUND (Meridian — deepest point)

The geometry has stopped being random. It has a direction — the wrong space pulls like a strong current. The party can feel where they're going.

The ley architecture wound: Bureau conduit lines running through the between-space. Visible. The harvest mechanism exposed — output flowing through the lines in the open. The party can see exactly what the ley architecture does and where the output goes.

Margot pulls the document case. Her consumption spikes — the numbers that never closed — close here. This is where the output she's been tracking has been going. One or two lines. She doesn't close the case.

Karim: the source of the entity-contact scarring in every patient he's ever treated. Not the Bureau's buildings above. This.

The wound has interactive points where the ley conduit lines can be examined up close. Touching them triggers a consequence: output drain on the party, a geometry spike. Players who heeded Kos's warning get a free pass.

**BOSS ENCOUNTER:** A major entity-adjacent phenomenon, drawn to the wound and feeding off it. The entity's presence is strongest here — the wound is a channel. The hardest encounter of Act 1.

*[ATB combat: Reid + Karim + Margot + Iris vs. major entity-adjacent phenomenon. Encounter design TBD.]*

**THE GLIMPSE:** After the fight. The party is catching its breath.

A figure at the far edge of the wound. The wrong geometry bends around her — not with her, around. In motion it reacts to air that isn't there. Platinum white hair. Near-black coat with a deep violet undertone.

The whole party sees her. Karim reads the entity-contact signature. Margot recognizes the description from her records. Iris sees someone wrong in a wrong place.

Reid sees Vesper.

The geometry between them resists being crossed — not a wall, just space that won't close. The player gets a prompted choice to pursue. The attempt fails. Whatever looks back from across the wound is aware.

She's gone before anyone crosses.

*[Story beat — Vesper's location and state confirmed. Not a confrontation. A sighting. Which presence looked back: unresolved.]*

The kid's geometry has stabilized slightly — the boss fight disrupted the pull temporarily. It won't hold. It bought them time.

Kos is still out there in the deep geometry. He helped without agreeing to. He's an open thread.

## Characters

### Kos — Occultist
See `CHARACTERS.md` for full entry.

Introduced here but does not join. Has been living in the deeper Meridian's wrong geometry — the only place in the city where what came through during his failed Output Calibration is quiet. He knows the Meridian better than anyone. He has felt knowledge of the wound without theoretical understanding of what it is. His mechanic (learns abilities from entity and entity-adjacent attacks) means this dungeon is where his transformation begins, even if the party doesn't know it yet.

His refusal to join is not hostility after the fight — it's the defensive logic of someone who has been running from the Bureau long enough to treat every contact as a potential trap. He is an open thread. He will surface again.

## Story Beats & Themes

**The machine made visible.** The harvest mechanism was always abstract — Margot's ledger, Karim's patients, Vesper's logs. The wound makes it physical. The party stands next to it and sees where the output goes. The horror stops being a theory.

**The compass needle and the source.** The kid's wrong geometry — what the party spent Case 2 managing — is revealed to be a symptom of something deeper, not its own cause. The party has been treating the response while the cause continued unopposed.

**Iris following the direction.** She identified this pull in Case 2 and refused to follow it alone for six months. Walking into it with the party is the arc's first movement — she chose this rather than being pushed. One brief beat acknowledges it. She doesn't make it a moment. It is one anyway.

**The first glimpse.** Reid has been looking for Vesper since Crane called. This is the closest he's gotten. The geometry between them won't close. Whatever looked back may not have been her. He doesn't have words for it yet.

## Connections & Foreshadowing

- **The wound's location** — now known to the party. Disrupting it would affect Kurtz's operation. Kurtz will eventually notice someone found it.
- **Kos as open thread** — he helped without joining. His mechanic means the encounters in this dungeon have already started changing him. The party doesn't know this yet. He doesn't either.
- **Vesper in the Meridian** — she's there. She knows Reid is looking. What comes next depends on which presence is in control when they meet again.
- **Margot's document case** — the gap in her ledger is now a physical location. The numbers close. What she does with that information is the next question.
- **The kid's geometry** — temporarily stabilized. The cause is still active. The window is short.
