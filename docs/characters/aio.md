# Āio — Martial Artist

**Class:** Martial Artist

**Bureau status:** Unmeterable — output loops inward as physical enhancement. Bureau assessment protocol requires outward resonance to function; their reading returns zero. Filed as non-practitioner, forgotten. They counted on this.

**Pronouns:** They/them

## Appearance
Māori / Pacific Islander, deep warm brown skin. Tall, visibly and powerfully muscular — the body of a fighter built through decades of practice, not Bureau training (Rhea Ripley register). Tā moko on chin and lower lip, worn openly. Natural black hair, long, worn in a warrior topknot — the upward silhouette is unique in the cast. Broad shoulders and defined arms with feminine softness in the face and bearing — androgynous, non-binary in presence as much as pronoun. Dark calm eyes that read as assessment rather than passivity. Loose dark sleeveless outer layers over darker underlayers, heavily worn. Bare arms — no cyberware, no implants. The absence of modification is the statement. Dominant color: **deep earth brown / bone white accents**.

**16-bit read:** Tā moko reads immediately as a distinctive facial marking at small scale. Tall/muscular silhouette with bare arms and upward topknot is completely distinct from every other character. The most physically imposing non-Bureau presence in the party.

## Background
From Māori / Pacific Islander island communities the Bureau never fully absorbed — the ley terminal network doesn't reach water well. The fighting tradition they carry predates the Bureau's entire framework: no sigils, no cyberware, not as a political position but because the tradition simply doesn't include external tools. The Bureau is irrelevant to what they practice.

The Bureau came to their community and ran an administrative sweep — not a crackdown. Forms, assessments, ley terminal installation, economic pressure. Made the community unlivable without integration. Āio watched it happen, couldn't stop it. Ended up in the Sprawl as part of the displacement. The dojo is grief made functional — a place that holds what the Bureau couldn't administer because it didn't know it existed.

## Core Wound
The slow administrative destruction of their community. The Bureau didn't hate it. It processed it. No villains, no violence — just the machine doing what the machine does, until the community was gone.

## The Tradition and the Entity
Āio's fighting tradition is completely orthogonal to the entity's machine — it predates the Bureau, predates WARDEN, predates the ley architecture. It was never contaminated by the system the entity built to manage people. This makes Āio the one party member who approaches the horror from a framework the entity has no model for — potentially the most dangerous perspective in the party by Act 3.
