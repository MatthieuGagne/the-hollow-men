# Soraya — Hacker

**Class:** Hacker

**Bureau status:** No file — always outside the system. Erases her own Grid activity logs. The Bureau knows someone goes deep. They don't know it's her.

## Appearance
Iranian/Persian, mid-to-late 50s, warm olive skin. Electric blue hair, long and loose — cold and digital against warm skin. Sharp precise features, lean, upright posture. Dark eyes with a faint Grid-glow in the iris, worn openly — the one tell she can't erase, made peace with it. A long white fitted tech coat, the only white clothing in the cast. Interface ports integrated at wrist and temple, glowing cold blue when she works. Dominant color: **white / cold blue accents**.

**16-bit read:** The only white coat in a cast of dark clothing — reads against every background and every party member. Electric blue long hair on warm olive skin is the sharpest warm/cool contrast in the cast. The Grid-glow is the accent in portrait close-ups.

## Background
Self-taught, no Academy, no Bureau formation. Has been in the deep Grid longer than most of the party has been alive. Was there when the Grid changed — when the entity's presence became felt at depth. Rather than pulling back, she went deeper. Methodically, carefully, over years and then decades. She has mapped the entity's information layer with more precision than anyone alive. She knows its shape without knowing its name. When Reid's investigation starts uncovering what's beneath NOX, she already has the map. She's been waiting for someone to ask the right questions.

## Bureau Status — The Erasure
She was never licensed or registered. What she erases are Grid activity logs — the passive signatures the Bureau's ley terminal network records whenever someone accesses the Grid at depth. Every deep dive leaves a trace. She's been systematically cleaning those traces for decades. The Bureau knows someone is going very deep. They don't know who. She intends to keep it that way.

## Mechanic
Hacks enemy cyberware and sigils to malfunction and backfire. Hacked enemies attack their own allies. Misfired sigils damage their casters. Disrupted systems cascade into adjacent systems. Deals almost no direct damage — the most indirect fighter in the party. The Grid is the vector; the enemy's own infrastructure is the weapon.

Distinct from Kos (Occultist): Kos absorbs what is done to him and turns it outward. Soraya turns the enemy's systems against themselves. Kos is passive acquisition; Soraya is active redirection.

## The Grid-Glow
Decades of neural interface have left a mark — a faint cold blue glow visible in her iris in certain light. She wears it openly. She's been outside the Bureau's system her whole life and has nothing to hide it from. It's the one tell she can't erase. She's made peace with it.

## Investigation Entry (Case 4 — Dead Signal, partial)

First contact is indirect. After the party defeats The Node at Junction 9, Margot reads the terminal's output log and finds Soraya's notation — advanced Grid-layer analysis syntax, a format beyond Bureau standard that Margot has never encountered. The message:

*"This terminal has been broadcasting for 94 days. You disrupted the activation but the pipeline is intact. I have mapped forty-seven terminals exhibiting this pattern across NOX. This is the first one that tried to push back. If you're reading this, you found it from the physical side. I found it from the other direction. I have the map. You have the investigation. — S."*

No location. No frequency. No meeting offered.

Soraya has been monitoring Junction 9 from the Grid side for months. She knows the party found it from the physical register. She has the Grid-layer map of the entity's information architecture across NOX — forty-seven terminals, decades of mapping. She does not join. She is an open thread.
