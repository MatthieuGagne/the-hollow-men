# Doc Karim — White Mage

**Class:** White Mage

**Bureau status:** Unlicensed — tolerated. The Sprawl needs healers. That arrangement ends the moment he associates with Reid's group. He knows this. He joins anyway.

**Handle:** Doc. Everyone calls him this. Karim is his actual name — on no file anywhere.

## Appearance
South Asian heritage (Pakistani or Bengali), warm medium-dark skin. Mid-50s, stocky build — the kind of man who looks like he's been carrying things for a long time. Dark hair going fully grey, worn loose and slightly unkempt. Dark warm eyes. A worn dark green utility jacket, heavily pocketed, patched in places, medical supplies — vials, syringes — visible on the exterior straps. A jury-rigged medical monocle over one eye: improvised cyberware with a faint diagnostic glow, built by someone who has been practicing without a license for decades. Dominant color: **deep worn green**.

## Background
Sprawl-born, Sprawl-stayed. Unlicensed community healer for decades — megacorp workers with industrial injuries, people who can't afford Bureau-licensed care, practitioners who've been calibrated and lost more than just their output. No Academy, no license, no Grade on file. The Bureau tolerates him because the Sprawl needs healers and he stays in his lane. He's never stayed in his lane. He's just never been worth the paperwork.

## Psychology
**Core wound:** He has been the cleanup crew for a machine he couldn't name for decades — treating the Bureau's damage one patient at a time, patching the aftermath, never touching the cause. Vesper is the crystallizing instance: he watched her change across years, gave her everything his medicine could offer, and lost her anyway.
**Coping:** Verbose, slightly over the top. He processes through talking. When something lands, he doesn't go quiet — he argues with it until it makes sense.
**Won't examine:** Whether staying in the Sprawl — taking anyone, asking no questions, working with what he has — was wisdom or a way of not having to fight the thing doing the damage.

## Investigation Entry (Case 1)
Reid brings Casimir Gemini to Karim's clinic — badly hurt from a Sprawl confrontation. Karim treats the surface injuries, but recognizes something underneath: entity-contact scarring. Not from the fight. Systemic. The same phenomenon he's been watching accumulate in a patient of his for years — arriving through a different vector. Casimir got there slowly, through decades of Bureau service and ley exposure. His patient got there fast, the Summoner version of the same damage.

While working, he names her: *"I've been treating someone with this same pattern. For years. Name was Vesper."*

Reid's comm rings. Old Bureau routing. Encrypted. Crane: *"Reid. It's Crane. Vesper's gone. I need you."*

Karim doesn't hear the call. When Reid ends it and turns back, he tells Karim enough: the woman he just named is missing. Karim's response is immediate and verbose — he makes his case, argues for himself, asks to join. He's been watching something happen to her for years without a category for it. He needs to understand what he was looking at. That need is stronger than what it costs him.

## Relationships
**Vesper:** Prior connection — treated her multiple times over years, beginning after Reid let her walk. She came first with ordinary injuries, then with presentations he couldn't categorize — entity exposure accumulating, getting worse each visit. She gave him pieces: Bureau-connected, dangerous, she was following something she couldn't look away from. He didn't push for more. She stopped coming. He filed it under *not my business*. He hasn't examined what he's afraid the silence means. The verbal fragments she gave him across visits surface as investigative leads later in the act, not as upfront exposition at recruitment.

Meets Reid in Case 1 through Casimir. Stranger to Reid and to everyone in the active party at entry. First to join after the opening sequence.
