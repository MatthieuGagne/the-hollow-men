# Character Differentiation

**Scope:** Reid, Iris, Margot, Gideon, Crane, Vesper
**Axes:** Physical (appearance, clothing, 16-bit silhouette), Psychological (wound, coping, blind spot), Naming

Additional inspirations confirmed this session: Twin Peaks, Control, Brazil, Akira, True Detective S1, N.K. Jemisin (Broken Earth), Walter Mosley, Kafka. See `docs/lore/concept.md` for full reference list.

## Physical Axis

### 16-Bit Design Principles
- **Silhouette is king** — every character readable by shape alone at small scale
- **Dark base, lighter mid-tone, one accent color** per character
- **Hair shape** matters more than hair color at pixel scale
- **One prop or distinctive element** per character as instant identifier

### Reid
**Silhouette:** Lean man in long coat, hands in pockets. Mid-height.
**Dominant color:** Amber / tobacco brown
**Skin:** Pale, Irish-Scottish descent. The pallor of a life lived at night in the rain.
**Hair:** Dark brown, slight widow's peak, grey at the temples. Short, self-cut.
**Eyes:** Dark grey.
**Clothing:** Long dark olive coat, worn lighter at the edges — Bureau-issue once, insignia scratched off the breast, ghost of it still visible. Plain dark shirt underneath, no tie (he used to wear one). Resoled leather boots.
**Accent:** Tarnished Bureau lighter — warm amber flame in dark scenes.
**16-bit read:** The coat silhouette + widow's peak are immediately readable. Amber lighter is the accent pop.

### Iris
**Silhouette:** Narrow, asymmetrical head shape. Close-fitted jacket, no volume.
**Dominant color:** Deep teal / dark grey-green
**Skin:** Warm medium tone, Southeast Asian heritage. Looks younger than she is.
**Hair:** Short asymmetrical red hair — longer on one side, shorter on the other. Dyed, chosen. Creates an instantly readable head silhouette.
**Eyes:** Dark, with a slight iridescent quality in certain light — the augmentation.
**Clothing:** Deep teal close-fitted jacket, all internal pockets, dark layered underlayers. Flat boots. Nothing external that could be grabbed. No jewelry, no accessories, nothing reflective.
**Accent:** Iridescent eye glow — subtle in world, punchy in battle portraits.
**16-bit read:** Asymmetrical red hair against teal jacket is the strongest color contrast in the cast. Immediately distinct.

### Margot
**Silhouette:** High collar framing the face, long straight hair falling on both sides. Geometric, controlled.
**Dominant color:** Deep burgundy / wine
**Skin:** Fair, Northern European. Academy pallor.
**Hair:** Long straight blonde hair, loose — the lightest hair in the cast, high contrast against the dark coat. When it's down and loose it's controlled precision. The Academy posture makes everything she wears look more formal than intended.
**Eyes:** Pale grey.
**Clothing:** Deep burgundy high-collared structured coat, fitted. The jailbroken sigil at her collarbone where the licensed one was — a faint cold glow, the one point of light on her. A worn leather document case.
**Accent:** The sigil glow at the collarbone — cold light, the only luminous point on an otherwise dark figure.
**16-bit read:** Long blonde hair + high collar is a clean distinctive silhouette. The sigil glow is the accent pop.

### Gideon
**Silhouette:** The widest, tallest shape in the cast. Immediately imposing.
**Dominant color:** Bureau blue / slate
**Skin:** Deep brown, rich dark complexion.
**Hair:** Close-cropped fade, short maintained beard. Bureau-regulation, never changed.
**Eyes:** Dark brown, warm but with threat assessment running underneath.
**Clothing:** Bureau slate-blue long coat, field-worn but cleaned after every operation. Gold Bureau badge on chest. Neural interface visible at his temple — Bureau-standard cyberware, uncovered. Heavy polished field boots. Scar on his jaw.
**Accent:** Gold Bureau badge — small warm glint against the cold blue. He puts it on every morning. It is a choice, not a habit.
**16-bit read:** The widest silhouette in the cast — impossible to confuse. Bureau blue is the most saturated color in the ensemble.

### Crane
**Silhouette:** Medium-narrow, slightly stooped from age and weight.
**Dominant color:** Charcoal grey / ash
**Skin:** Mixed Black and white heritage, medium-dark weathered skin.
**Hair:** White, close-cropped — the Bureau regulation cut, thirty years after retirement, still maintained. Short grey beard.
**Eyes:** Warm brown, tired in a specific way.
**Clothing:** Dark charcoal long coat, still pressed. Warm grey-brown sweater underneath. The coat was Bureau-adjacent once. Now it is just a coat he cannot stop maintaining.
**16-bit read:** White hair against charcoal coat is high contrast. The slight stoop distinguishes his silhouette from Gideon's upright mass.

### Vesper
**Silhouette:** The softest outline in the cast — loose natural hair against the city's hard geometry.
**Dominant color:** Deep violet / near-black
**Skin:** Medium brown — Crane's daughter, the heritage visible but sitting differently.
**Hair:** Platinum white, loose natural texture. Congenital — she was always marked this way. In motion it reacts to air that isn't there.
**Eyes:** Dark brown in most light. In violet neon the color shifts in a way the lighting cannot explain.
**Clothing:** Near-black coat with a deep violet undertone that only appears in neon light. Multiple dark layers that don't hang quite right. She was dressing to disappear before she disappeared.
**Accent:** The violet undertone catching in neon — she almost disappears in darkness, then the color finds her.
**16-bit read:** Platinum white hair is the most distinctive head in the cast. The near-black coat makes her face and hair the only visible elements.

## Psychological Axis

### Reid
**Core wound:** He did the right thing, it cost him everything, and he'd do it again. The certainty is the problem — not the regret.
**Coping:** Dark humor is one layer among others. Not his defining register — to be fleshed out fully in dialogue.
**Won't examine:** Whether the life he's built since the disgrace is actually living.

### Iris
**Core wound:** She's run so many covers for so long she doesn't know what's underneath. The question exists. She doesn't ask it.
**Coping:** Pure competence. If she keeps moving she doesn't have to stop and look.
**Won't examine:** Why she stayed for the kid.

### Margot
**Core wound:** She was the instrument of her own loss. She filed the form correctly.
**Coping:** Intellectual framework — she rebuilt ideology around the grief. Genuine, and also a way of not sitting with the guilt directly.
**Won't examine:** Whether the jailbreak was justice or self-punishment.

### Gideon
**Core wound:** His entire identity is organized around an institution that doesn't deserve it.
**Coping:** Procedure. The report, the protocol, the form. Not bureaucracy — faith.
**Won't examine:** What his silence about Margot's report makes him.

### Crane
**Core wound:** He taught Reid everything, and Reid is the only one who actually acted on it.
**Coping:** Retrospection — parcels out what he knows based on what he thinks people can carry.
**Won't examine:** Whether protecting people from the truth is care or control. **Resolved:** He loses this argument. There is a scene where Reid discovers Crane was managing him. The mentor who couldn't stop being a handler.

### Vesper
**Core wound:** She followed a thread because she trusted something, and it led somewhere she can't come back from.
**Coping:** Compartmentalization — the original Vesper kept things separate. The entity doesn't cope. It simply is.
**Won't examine:** N/A. She found the truth. That's the problem.
**Note:** Two presences, one face. The original Vesper and the entity. Every scene is a question of which one is speaking.

## Key Relationship Dynamics

**Reid / Gideon** — True Detective S1 spine. Two men with radically different relationships to truth, stuck working the same case. Reid has looked at it and can't unsee it. Gideon manages what he looks at.

**Iris / Margot** — Friction first, alliance earned. Iris distrusts ideology because she's never needed one — she just acts. Margot rebuilt her identity around one, constructed from grief. That gap creates early tension before it becomes the thing that makes them work together.

**Crane / Reid** — The mentor who was always also a handler. Crane gives Reid the case without the weight of what he knows. Reid finds out. That costs them something.

**Vesper / everyone** — The question of which presence is speaking is always present in her scenes.
