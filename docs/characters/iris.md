# Iris — Grey-Market Fixer

**Class:** Thief (high PSY)
**Bureau status:** Never registered
**Grade:** None. Bureau instruments return an error before a number.
**Handle:** Iris. Not her real name. She's had it long enough that she doesn't remember when she stopped thinking of it as a cover. Real name unknown.
**First encounter:** Beat 3 of the opening sequence — already in the building, already working, already done before Reid knows who she is. She takes the jailbroken sigils and ghosts clean. No name. No thread. Just the sound of a panel sliding back into place.

**What she is:** Moves jailbroken sigils — Bureau-registered hardware with the ley return channel cut — to people who need to stop being metered. Off-grid from birth. No license, no file, no record. The Bureau has nothing on her because it has nothing *of* her.

**Why she's stronger than licensed practitioners:** She was never metered. Her psychic output has never been split — the Bureau's harvest system never got installed. She has no Grade because no ley terminal ever got a clean read on her; she was never near one long enough, never enrolled, never processed. She's been operating at full output her whole life without knowing the gap exists. She doesn't have a theory about it. She's never needed one. When the revelation comes, she'll understand the system she's been fighting without knowing what it was.

---

## The Runner

Iris runs every morning. Rain, no rain, 3am after a job, doesn't matter. It's not fitness. It's the one honest thing she does — the body enacting what the rest of her life is for.

She runs from her emotions, her problems, herself. Not because she's weak. Because she learned young that stopping is how you get caught.

## Background

Poor family. Debtors at the door before she was old enough to understand what debt was. Survival mode isn't a coping strategy she developed — it's the water she was raised in. The covers, the handles, the constant motion: all of it started as literal necessity before it became identity.

She's been running so long she doesn't know what she'd find if she stopped. She suspects it would be bad. She doesn't intend to find out.

## The Facade

Iris is funny. Genuinely, sharply funny — the kind of wit that can cut someone open and make them laugh about it. She speaks her mind in full, she doesn't do clipped. She likes parties, loud music, crowded rooms where nobody expects anything from her but a good time.

She is also a music obsessive. She listens to dense, synthetic music — something you can disappear into, not a statement, not political. Pure escape. She is also a fan of Paz specifically, whose work she knows from the Sprawl underground before they ever meet. Paz doesn't know Iris exists.

None of this is fake, exactly. The party-girl and the operative are the same person in different registers — the facade is real enough that she believes it herself. But it's load-bearing in a way she doesn't examine. Keep it light, keep moving, and the darkness stays in the periphery where it belongs.

## Lain

A girl. Fourteen, maybe — hard to say with street kids, the years sit differently. Iris found her being attacked by a gang, stepped in, and then couldn't walk away. She doesn't do charity. She made one exception and she's been living with it for eighteen months.

She won't say why. She knows why. Lain is her at fourteen — same city, same margins, same nothing to fall back on. Staying for Lain is staying for herself, which means looking at herself, which she doesn't do.

Lain knows about her anomalous output and wants to understand it. She's at the stage where she butts heads with any authority — in this case, Iris. Iris has no toolkit for being the authority figure she spent her whole life running from. When Lain pushes back, Iris tries humor first, then goes cold and operational, then goes for a run. The argument ends without resolving. Neither calls it what it is.

Lain is already hardening into the same armor Iris built. Iris watches it happen. She knows exactly where it goes.

Before joining Reid's party, Iris takes Lain to her apartment — the address she's never given anyone. The invitation and Lain's location are the same act of trust. She doesn't name it.

Lain's anomalous unregistered output resonates with the between-space, pulling the Meridian's wrong geometry worse each week. Iris has been managing the escalation alone.

## Voice

She speaks her mind before she's done thinking it through — the wit is fast, the self-awareness is real, and she'll use both edges of it without warning. She doesn't do earnest declarations. She does long swerving sentences that arrive somewhere true while making it look accidental.

On the rare occasion something actually lands on her — really lands — she'll make a joke first. Then another one. Then go quiet. The quiet is the tell.

Her backstory stays subtext. The word "debtors" never appears in dialogue. Everything about where she came from is readable in how she treats Lain.

## Psychology

**Core wound:** She's run so many covers for so long she doesn't know what's underneath. The question exists. She doesn't ask it.

**Coping:** Keep moving. Keep it funny. Don't stop long enough for anything to catch up. Morning runs. Packed social calendar. The job always comes first because the job requires full attention, and full attention means she doesn't have to think.

**Won't examine:** Why she stayed for Lain. She knows. That's why.

## Dynamic: Iris and Margot

Friction before alliance. Iris reads Margot's ideology as something only someone who was inside the machine can afford — you build a moral framework around the damage you did after you've had the privilege of being processed. Iris was never near it. She survived it from the outside.

Margot finds Iris's deflection irresponsible. Iris finds Margot's earnestness exhausting in the specific way that things you secretly respect are exhausting.

The gap closes when each recognizes the other has a person they'd burn it all down for. Neither says so. They don't need to.

## Dynamic: Iris and Paz

Natural ground — Iris is a music obsessive and already a fan of Paz's work before they meet. The asymmetry: Iris knows who Paz is. Paz has never heard of Iris. The moment Paz realizes this is a scene.

They argue about music (Iris's synthetic/escape register vs. Paz's raw political guitar). They understand each other anyway. Paz becomes an unconscious translator between Iris and Margot — the warmth that makes the friction livable.

## Investigation Entry (Case 2 — Beats 10–15)

Reid's investigation doesn't connect to Iris through Lain's history — it connects through the anomaly Lain is generating. All three data sets (Vesper's apartment logs, Margot's disappearance cohort records, Karim's patient files) independently point to the same pocket in the Meridian. Reid's party arrives during an escalation Iris can no longer manage alone. She joins because the investigation is already inside her problem and there's no separating them. She hates that.

Her contribution: the anomaly has a direction, pulling toward something specific deeper in the Meridian — she's been watching it for six months without following it. Before joining, she takes Lain to her apartment — the address she's never given anyone. Then she invites the party. The invitation and Lain's location are the same act of trust.

## What She Doesn't Know

Anything about WARDEN, the entity, or the ley architecture's actual function. She comes into the main mystery clean — outside perspective. Asks the questions an outsider would ask. When she says she doesn't know what she walked into, she says so.

## Appearance

Southeast Asian heritage, warm medium skin tone — looks younger than she is. Short asymmetric hair, bright red — vivid, not dark — dramatically cut: jaw-length on one side, nearly shaved on the other. Dark eyes with a striking iridescent quality in neon light — clearly artificial, beautiful, like oil on dark water or a cephalopod's eye catching color — a permanent augmentation she can't remove and has made peace with. The one tell she cannot erase. A matte deep teal catsuit, form-fitting, high collar, hood worn down at the neck — the hidden pockets visible only as faint panel seam lines along the torso. Copper accent at seam piping, collar edge, and pocket panel borders — warm metallic against cool teal. Flat-soled integrated boots, one continuous silhouette. Nothing external that could be grabbed. No jewelry, no accessories, nothing reflective. Dominant color: **deep teal / copper accent**.

## Iris's Code

She screens every client. Not about the sigil — about what they'll do with the freed output. She asks. She listens. She's been wrong twice. She carries that.
