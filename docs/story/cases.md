# Cases

## Case 1: The Recovery Job

### Overview

Reid is hired by Assessment Division contact Holloway to locate a missing practitioner, Vera Gemini. Vera has gone AWOL. The official story is sigil reinstatement, but Holloway's real interest is bringing her in. During the recovery, Reid discovers Vera is hiding with Iris (the fixer from the opening). A confrontation in the Sprawl escalates when territory enforcers arrive. Reid and Iris fight together; Vera's father Casimir is badly hurt. Reid secures the sigil and brings Vera to Holloway for payment. But before delivering her, Vera begs Reid to help her father. Reid agrees, bringing Casimir to Doc Karim in the Sprawl. When Karim treats Casimir's injuries, he recognizes something systemic: entity-contact scarring from Casimir's years of Bureau service. Karim realizes he's dealing with something larger than individual medical cases. He joins Reid.

---

### Beat Map

#### BEAT 1 — THE OFFER (Four Winds Bar)

Holloway arrives at Reid's usual table. Professional, cold, all business. Assessment Division Gray — someone from Reid's past, someone who was there the night Reid let Vesper walk. The veiled threat is implicit: *you owe the Bureau, and we're calling it in.*

**Holloway's pitch:**
- Vera Gemini, Grade II practitioner, has gone AWOL
- Bureau wants her back for reinstatement
- Official language: sigil recovery, bureaucratic process
- Actual interest: bringing her in
- Holloway doesn't care about details or methodology
- Just results

**Subtext:** Holloway knows Reid has history. Knows he'll understand what "clean up the mess" means. Knows he'll take the job because he needs money and because refusing a Gray isn't something Reid can afford.

---

#### BEAT 2 — THE HUNT

Reid pieces together where Vera is. He retraces his steps from the opening case: Vera owes Suzy money. Suzy is a money lender who operates out of a back-room network in the Heights. Reid knows how to find her.

**The investigation:**
- Reid approaches Suzy at the Four Winds or at her operation
- Suzy doesn't want to talk (client confidentiality, fear)
- Reid leverages: *Vera's missing, Bureau's involved, her family's in danger*
- Suzy gives up the lead: Vera reached out to a fixer, went to ground in the Sprawl

**The discovery:**
- The fixer is Iris — the woman from the opening
- She collects jailbroken sigils and helps people drop off Bureau grid
- Vera is hiding with her
- Reid realizes: Vera's been running since the opening. Iris recognized her sigil description and reached out, offered help

**Timeline:** A day or two of legwork. Reid's good at this.

---

#### BEAT 3 — THE CONVERGENCE

Multiple people converging on the same location in the Sprawl:

**Reid** arrives hunting for Vera (Bureau job, paycheck pending)

**Casimir Gemini** has found the safehouse independently. He's desperate — his daughter is on the run, his family's reputation is collapsing. He's looking for Vera, maybe looking for the sigil to prove she can be reinstated. He doesn't know Iris is involved. He just knows his daughter is somewhere in the Sprawl.

**Vera and Iris** are in a safehouse/meeting location. Iris is exploring options: can Vera be jailbroken like other practitioners? Can she stay off-grid? What are the costs?

**Tension in the room:**
- Reid enters as Bureau muscle (from Vera and Iris's perspective)
- Casimir recognizes Reid (from Heights circles, or Bureau association)
- Vera is caught between her father trying to save her and a stranger working for the Bureau
- Iris assesses: *can we fight this, can we run, what's the play?*

No violence yet. Just pressure mounting.

---

#### BEAT 4 — THE ERUPTION

Sprawl territory enforcers arrive. This is their turf — they shake down operations for control and profit. They don't know who's in the safehouse. They just know there's activity, and they want payment.

**The fight:**
- Enforcers kick in, immediate escalation
- Reid and Iris, by necessity, fight together (like the opening)
- Casimir tries to protect Vera or hold ground; gets caught in the chaos
- Badly hurt by enforcer weapons/techniques (not by Iris, not by Reid — collateral damage)
- Multiple rounds of escalating violence as more enforcers arrive

**During the fight:**
- Someone (Iris? Vera?) yells about the sigil
- Reid pushes toward it, secures it in the chaos
- Iris gets Vera toward an exit

**The aftermath:**
- Enforcers are overwhelmed or retreated
- Iris and Vera escape into the Sprawl's tangle
- Reid has Casimir (badly hurt) and the sigil
- Iris is gone (for the second time)

---

#### BEAT 5 — THE DEAL

Reid finds Vera before she fully vanishes with Iris. Vera is panicked, torn between running and helping her father.

**Vera's desperation:**
- "Please. He's hurt. He tried to help me. Please."
- She's begging Reid to get Casimir medical care
- She knows what comes next: Bureau pickup, questioning, reinstatement or worse

**Reid's choice:**
- He needs the money (Holloway's job)
- But he sees a dying man and a desperate woman
- He knows what it costs to do the right thing
- He agrees: *I'll get him help. Then I deliver you to Holloway.*

**Iris's exit:**
- Iris sees Reid keeping his word to Vera
- She nods once, recognizes something
- She takes one last look at Vera, then ghosts into the Sprawl
- (Third time Iris vanishes; sets up her eventual return as 4th party member)

---

#### BEAT 6 — THE SPRAWL

Reid carries or guides Casimir into the deeper Sprawl. This is unfamiliar territory for him — he's Heights-born, Heights-trained. The Sprawl feels wrong in different ways than the Heights. More human mess, less engineered wrongness.

He asks around. Finds Doc Karim's operation through street contacts or word-of-mouth. A healer who asks no questions. Takes anyone. Works with what he has.

---

#### BEAT 7 — THE DIAGNOSIS

Karim's clinic. Jury-rigged, clean enough, filled with people too poor or too broken to go Bureau-licensed.

Karim begins treating Casimir's injuries. Surface wounds from the fight. But as he works, he sees something underneath.

**Entity-contact scarring.** Not from the fight. From years of Bureau service. Systemic ley exposure at the cellular level — something about the Bureau's apparatus changing tissue at a level medical science doesn't have categories for.

But Karim has seen this before. Not in Bureau enforcers. In a patient who came back to his clinic again and again across years, each visit a little worse than the last. A Summoner. The same damage, different vector: she got there fast where Casimir got there slow.

He keeps working. Thinking out loud:

**Karim:** *"I've been treating someone with this same pattern. For years. Name was Vesper."*

Reid's comm rings. Old Bureau routing. Encrypted.

**Crane:** *"Reid. It's Crane. Vesper's gone. I need you."*

The player has heard that name twice in ten seconds — from a Sprawl healer following a medical thread, and from an encrypted call on old Bureau routing. Karim doesn't hear the call. He's still working.

---

#### BEAT 8 — THE HOOK

Reid ends the call. Turns back. Tells Karim enough:

**Reid:** *"The woman you just named. She's missing. Someone I know needs me to find her."*

Karim stops working. Stands up. Makes his case — verbose, urgent, slightly over the top:

**Karim:** *"That woman — Vesper — I've been treating her for years. Years. The tissue presentations, the pattern in this man's body — it's the same thing, do you understand? I have been watching this happen and I have had no category for it. No category. I need to understand what I've been looking at. I need to understand what happened to her. Take me with you."*

He's in. Whatever it costs — the Bureau's tolerance, the arrangement that's kept him safe for decades — he's already decided.

---

### The Characters

#### Vera Gemini
**Grade II practitioner, Heights family**

- Multi-generational Bureau service (mother, father both served)
- Her sigil has been draining for months — faster than normal because of family bloodline saturation
- Can't work, can't earn money
- Owes medical debt to Suzy for her father's treatment
- Desperate, frightened, but not broken
- Reaches out to Iris (the fixer) when she goes AWOL
- Begs Reid to help her father before the Bureau takes her

#### Casimir Gemini
**Vera's father, former Bureau enforcer**

- Multi-generational Bureau service (his mother before him)
- Driven by family pride and shame
- Already sick with entity-contact scarring from years of Bureau work
- Tries to recover Vera's sigil himself to "save family face"
- Gets badly hurt in the Sprawl confrontation
- Angry at Vera for the debt, for the disgrace, for putting them in this position
- His body carries decades of ley exposure that Karim will recognize

#### Holloway
**Assessment Division Gray**

- Former colleague of Reid's
- Was present (or knows about) the night Reid let Vesper walk
- Uses veiled threat rather than direct coercion
- Professional, cold, no unnecessary words
- Doesn't care about details or methodology
- Just wants results

#### Suzy
**Money lender, Heights operation**

- Vera's creditor
- Professional, no sympathy
- Just wants her money
- Woman, operates a network
- First meets Reid at the Four Winds Bar

---

### Story Beats & Themes

**Spiral inward:** Case 1 moves from a personal job (find one woman) to recognition of a systemic problem (ley exposure, Bureau machinery).

**Generational weight:** The Gemini family's service to the Bureau is what's destroying them. Loyalty doesn't protect you from the machine.

**Necessity makes allies:** Reid and Iris work together again. They don't trust each other, but survival demands cooperation.

**The cost of doing right:** Reid agrees to help Casimir despite it costing him time and attention. It's the move that costs disgraced enforcers everything. He does it anyway.

**Karim's entry:** The healer who's been patching up the system's victims finally sees the system itself.

---

### Connections & Foreshadowing

- **Entity-contact scarring** appears in Casimir's body. This will become a major revelation: the Bureau's ley architecture doesn't just harvest power, it leaves traces in those who serve it.
- **Generational exposure** — the more a family is integrated with the Bureau, the worse the drain. This hints at something deeper about bloodlines and ley connection.
- **Iris's protection of Vera** — Iris has no reason to help. She just does. This sets up later revelations about why Iris is so good at disappearing, why she understands the system so well.
- **Holloway's interest in Vera specifically** — why does the Bureau suddenly want this one practitioner? TBD, but it connects to Vesper's disappearance and the larger mystery.

## Case 2: The Document Case

### Overview

Reid and Karim follow Crane's first Vesper lead — her last known address in the Heights. The Grays have already swept it. What remains are ley terminal output logs Vesper left intentionally. Simultaneously, a Gold Coat operation is running a silent hunt across two districts for a fugitive Black Mage pulling classified calibration records. Both threads converge at the Four Winds Bar. Margot joins. Kurtz is introduced, unrecognized.

---

### Beat Map

#### BEAT 1 — THE FIRST LEAD (Four Winds Bar)

Crane meets Reid. Off the record. He gives Reid Vesper's last known address in the Heights — an apartment maintained under a false name, a thread only Crane knew about. He doesn't know what's there. He knows she left something behind intentionally.

---

#### BEAT 2 — THE ADDRESS (Heights — Vesper's Apartment)

Reid and Karim investigate. Investigation mode — search the apartment, read the space. Vesper stripped it deliberately but left one thing: ley terminal output logs, printed and annotated in her hand. Dense technical notation. Karim can't fully parse it. Reid can't either. Someone with Academy formation would know what they're looking at.

The apartment has been swept — Bureau Gray footprints, recent. Someone got here first and missed the logs.

---

#### BEAT 3 — THE SWEEP (Heights — Stairwell / Street)

The Grays are still in the building. Not here for Reid — not yet — but his presence triggers assessment. A Gray team, professional, Bureau-trained. And at the edge of the operation: a figure in a gold coat, observing from the far end of the corridor. Reid sees the team. Sees the coat. Files it and moves.

The Gray team moves to intercept. Hot Zone.

*ATB combat: Reid + Karim vs. Gray team — Gray Field Agent(s), Gray Assessor(s). First direct conflict with Bureau enforcement. The gold coat withdraws before Reid can close.*

---

#### BEAT 4 — THE THREAD (Sprawl — transit)

Moving fast. Karim reads the apartment logs in fragments — he recognizes the pattern. Entity-contact scarring isn't just in bodies. Vesper was tracking the same accumulation in the ley architecture's output records. Someone else has been building a parallel case from the calibration records angle.

He gives Reid a name: *"She called herself Margot. Academy-trained. She came to my clinic once, asking about calibrated patients I'd treated. Left quickly. I don't have a file on her."*

One of Karim's patients — a calibrated Grade III, vanished six months ago — fits the pattern in Vesper's logs exactly.

---

#### BEAT 5 — THE SAFEHOUSE TRAIL (Sprawl — two locations)

Reid traces Margot's movement through Sprawl channels. Two safehouses in the last month. The second: empty, recently vacated, evidence of sustained research work. Printouts. Academy-trained notation on every page. The Bureau got here first — the space was tossed. They were looking for the document case. They didn't find it.

---

#### BEAT 6 — THE CORDON (Sprawl — street level)

The Gray operation has widened into a sweep. Multiple encounters, escalating — the Grays operating outside their Heights jurisdiction, improvising, which makes them less predictable. During the fight: a ley terminal in this block pulses once, wrong. Karim notices.

*ATB combat: Reid + Karim vs. Gray sweep teams — multi-encounter escalation.*

---

#### BEAT 7 — THE BAR (Four Winds — Margot arrives)

They make it back to the Four Winds. Reid is processing: the apartment logs, Karim's patient, a Gold Coat running a two-district operation — this is bigger than a fugitive jailbreaker.

Margot sits down beside him. Not across. The document case is open on the table before she says a word.

*"You helped Vesper walk. I need to know why she was worth the cost. Because I think whatever she found is why these people are gone."*

The conversation: Reid asks his questions. She gives the data — the disappearance cohort, the output surplus, the consumption spikes. Karim recognizes his patient in her records. Three people looking at the same machine from three different angles.

The Gold Coat's team finds the bar.

---

#### BEAT 8 — THE BAR FIGHT (Four Winds — Hot Zone)

The same gold coat from Beat 3 — closer now. This time it's not assessment. The document case is on the table and they want it.

Margot and Reid fight together — forced party. She fights with what the Academy gave her: precise, controlled, Grade IV technique. When her limit break lands, it's something the Academy didn't teach — still learning its own shape. Raw.

The Gold Coat observes from the back of the room. Does not engage. Reid sees the face in the crowd — a scar through the left brow, catching the bar light. He files it. Doesn't place it. The gold coat is wrong, the rank is wrong, twenty years is a long time. The coat withdraws when the fight turns.

*ATB combat: Reid + Karim + Margot (guest) vs. Gray enforcement team. Gold Coat Operative (Kurtz) present; observes, does not engage.*

---

#### BEAT 9 — THE DECISION (Four Winds — aftermath)

Margot could leave. She has what she came for — Reid is pursuing Vesper, the investigation is converging on the same machine she's been mapping.

She doesn't.

*"The Grays just ran a sweep across two Sprawl districts for a fugitive jailbreaker with a document case. That's not normal resourcing. Whatever I found — you need to understand it before they get it back. And I need to understand what Vesper found. We're working the same case."*

She's in.

---

#### BEAT 10 — THE CONVERGENCE (Four Winds — story beat)

Margot reads Vesper's apartment logs — she has the Academy formation to parse the notation. What they reveal: all three data sets are tracking the same anomalous output signature.

- **Vesper's logs:** ley terminal output readings from across NOX, with one cluster of anomalous consumption in the Meridian that doesn't match any registered practitioner. Vesper circled it. No annotation — just the circle. She noticed it and didn't know what to make of it.
- **Margot's records:** the same geographic cluster appears as a consumption spike her disappearance cohort accounting can't assign to any calibration case. The output is being drawn toward that pocket but it isn't coming from a Bureau-registered source. A gap in her ledger she couldn't explain.
- **Karim's files:** a patient who lived near the Meridian border described symptoms consistent with proximity to a sustained output source — headaches, disorientation, geometry shifting. Karim noted it as environmental exposure, filed the location. Same pocket.

Three instruments, same signal. One pocket in the Meridian. The geometry there has been degrading for months.

---

#### BEAT 11 — THE THRESHOLD (Meridian entry — exploration + first encounter)

First time the party enters the Meridian. Wrong geometry is immediate and real: streets that don't connect the way they should, a doorway opening onto a room that can't exist behind the wall, rain falling at an angle the wind doesn't explain. Not surreal — wrong in a way that has a logic to it that resists being named.

Investigation Mode works differently here. The environment doesn't read the way a room reads. Things leave impressions that don't match their physical form.

First entity-adjacent enemies introduced. Not Bureau, not enforcers. Phenomena — things from between-space bleeding through where the membrane is thinnest. The encounter is jarring by design: different logic, nothing the party's Bureau experience prepared them for.

*ATB combat: Reid + Karim + Margot vs. entity-adjacent phenomena — Shade(s). Tutorial encounter for entity-adjacent enemy category.*

---

#### BEAT 12 — GOING DEEPER (Meridian interior — dungeon escalation)

The geometry worsens as they approach the signature. The wrong architecture is directional now — the space resists being moved through. Entity-adjacent encounters escalate.

Karim reads the walls. The entity-contact scarring he's seen in Casimir's body, in his patients — but in the building material. Architecture that's been ley-exposed long enough that the damage is structural. He has a category for what he's looking at. Having the category is worse than not having one.

*ATB combat: multi-encounter escalation — Shade(s), Vessel(s). Wrong geometry as environmental hazard.*

---

#### BEAT 13 — FINDING IRIS (Meridian pocket — story beat)

The party finds the kid first: a teenager, androgynous, Sprawl-born, sitting in the eye of the anomaly. Name TBD — street-given, not registered. Entity-touched — early stage, the consumption slow but ongoing. Their unmetered output resonates with the between-space as the entity draws at them, which is why the geometry has been worsening. They've been here for weeks. Every attempt to leave makes it worse. The entity-touch is already visible if you know what you're looking at: the pallor starting, the eyes catching the neon strangely, small things forgotten in conversation and retrieved a beat late.

Iris is between the kid and the party. Not waiting — managing. She's been holding the escalation alone, and it's been winning.

She clocks Reid. Assessment (threat?) then recognition (twice before — the opening, Case 1). She lets them through. Says nothing about it.

---

#### BEAT 14 — THE BREAK (Meridian pocket — major encounter)

The party's presence tips the balance — four people, three unmetered practitioners, enough output in the pocket to trigger a larger phenomenon. Something from the between-space fully crosses. The biggest encounter of Case 2.

Iris fights as a full guest party member for the first time — not the controlled chaos of the opening or the safehouse. Her element.

*ATB combat: Reid + Karim + Margot + Iris (guest) vs. The Crossing (entity boss). First full demonstration of Iris's combat class.*

---

#### BEAT 15 — THE RECRUITMENT (Meridian pocket — aftermath)

The geometry is quiet. Temporarily. The kid is stable. Temporarily.

Iris could move them now. She could ghost for a fourth time. Instead she starts talking — not about joining, about the situation: what she's seen the geometry do, what the kid's output has been doing, what Vesper's logs mean in the context of what she's watched happen here for eighteen months.

The key: the anomaly has a *direction*. Not just worsening — pulling toward something specific, deeper in the Meridian. She's been resisting following it for six months. The party's three data sets explain what the anomaly is. Iris tells them where it's going.

*"I've been watching it pull that way for six months. I didn't follow it because I didn't want to know what was at the end. You're going to find out anyway."*

She takes the kid to her apartment — the address she's never given anyone. Then, to the party: *"You can use my place."* Flat. Practical. One line. The invitation and the kid's location are the same act of trust.

She stays because the investigation is inside her problem and there's no separating them. She hates that. She doesn't say so. The party can tell.

*Iris joins the party. Party is now Reid + Karim + Margot + Iris.*

---

#### BEAT 16 — THE APARTMENT (Iris's place, deep Sprawl — hub introduction)

A residential floor in a dense Sprawl block. From the hallway it looks like one apartment among hundreds. Bureau instruments pass over it.

Inside: domestic surface — main room, kitchen alcove, short corridor. The kid is already there. Iris called ahead; nobody heard the call. She moves through her own space with the economy of someone who has lived alone in it for a long time and is now making a calculation she's already made.

At some point a door opens — the one that looks like a closet. Behind it: off-grid data routing, a workshop surface, a terminal that doesn't touch the Bureau's ley architecture at any registered node. She doesn't explain it. She needed something from inside.

Margot finds the kitchen table. Opens the document case. That's her spot now.

Karim claims a corner of the main room. He has opinions about the lighting.

Reid pulls the nearest chair. Doesn't ask whose it is.

The kid's room is off the corridor. Their name — street-given, not registered — is said once, naturally, when Iris introduces them. First time we hear it.

*Hub activates. Player has control — explore the space, trigger first-impression dialogue. Rest/Save and Party Management now available.*

---

### Story Beats & Themes

**Two investigations, one machine.** Reid is following Vesper forward. Margot has been following the calibration program's damage backward. They meet in the middle — the same machine, different entry points.

**The Gold Coat as wrongness.** In a world of dark nocturnal uniforms, a full gold coat is aggressive, impossible. The Bureau defers to it without knowing why. That deference is the horror made visible.

**Necessity makes allies again.** Reid and Margot fight together before either chooses it — the same dynamic as Reid and Iris in the opening, Reid and Iris again in Case 1. The pattern is structural. Alliances in this city form under fire.

**Karim's role expands.** He is the thread connecting both investigations — he treated calibrated practitioners (Margot's data), he treated Casimir (entity-contact scarring), he named Vesper (the Vesper case). He is the party's institutional memory of the machine's damage.

---

### Connections & Foreshadowing

- **Kurtz's gold coat** — first appearance of a WARDEN designation in the physical world. The player doesn't know what it means yet. Karim notices the ley terminal pulse during Beat 6. The two things are connected.
- **Margot's document case** — the ledger of the disappearance cohort will become a key investigation tool. The gaps in her records (cases she can't access) are what the party will fill across Act 1.
- **Vesper's apartment logs** — Margot can read them. Together with Margot's records and Karim's files they converge on the Meridian pocket — the same anomalous signature from three angles.
- **The anomaly's direction** — Iris has been watching the geometry pull toward something deeper in the Meridian for six months. What's at the end of that direction is the next investigation thread.
- **The face Reid files** — Kurtz, unrecognized, at the Four Winds. A gold coat, a scar through the left brow. Reid put that scar there the night Vesper walked. When the recognition comes later — the distance finally closing, the scar finally placing the face — it reframes everything in Case 2 retroactively.

## Case 3: The Wound

### Overview

The party descends into the deeper Meridian following the direction of the anomaly Iris identified in Case 2. Wrong geometry escalates across four zones. Kos — a Bureau escapee hiding in the deep geometry, entity-touched from a failed Output Calibration — is encountered in Zone 3 and does not join. The dungeon ends at a ley architecture wound: Bureau conduit lines running through the between-space, the harvest mechanism physically exposed. Margot's document case data closes here. Vesper is glimpsed at the far edge of the wound. The party returns to the hub changed.

---

### Beat Map

#### BEAT 1 — THE HUB: SETTLING IN (Iris's apartment)

The party has arrived. Character positions are established through exploration and first-impression dialogue: Margot at the kitchen table, document case open. Karim in his corner — he has opinions about the lighting. Iris in the workshop, door open now. Reid with no fixed spot.

The kid's entity-touch tells are present. Pallor that shouldn't be there — too young to look this drained. Eyes that catch the neon strangely, the glow faint but there. A small forgetting in the first conversation, smoothed over, retrieved a beat late. Iris manages this matter-of-factly — she's been doing it alone for eighteen months. Nothing in her manner suggests it's alarming. It is alarming.

---

#### BEAT 2 — THE SYNTHESIS (Iris's apartment — kitchen table)

Margot, Karim, and Iris cross-reference at the kitchen table. The three data sets converged on the Meridian pocket in Case 2 — but the pocket was the kid's resonance responding to something deeper, not generating it. Vesper's logs, Margot's records, Karim's files all pointed to a symptom. The source is further in.

The key discovery: Karim notices the kid's entity-touch symptoms have been worsening since the party arrived. More unmetered practitioners in the apartment, more output in the space, stronger pull from whatever is deeper in the Meridian. The pallor is deeper than a week ago. The forgettings are longer. The kid is a compass needle — pointing at the source, not being it.

---

#### BEAT 3 — THE TRIGGER (Iris's apartment)

The kid's symptoms spike — worst they've been. Pallor visibly deeper, the eye glow brighter, a longer gap before the next sentence. Something deeper in the Meridian is pulling harder. Iris reads it immediately: it's been building since the party arrived. It won't stop building.

They can't wait.

---

### Zone 1 — The Threshold (Meridian — just past Case 2 pocket)

Familiar enough to navigate, wrong enough to feel wrong. The geometry is off in ways the party has seen before. Margot can read it with Academy notation — she has the formation to parse space that doesn't behave. First entity-adjacent encounters. First loot caches.

*ATB combat: lower-tier entity-adjacent phenomena — Shade(s). Tutorial zone for dungeon mechanics.*

---

### Zone 2 — The Architecture (Meridian — intermediate depth)

Streets that don't connect as they should. Mid-tier encounters. Karim reads the building material: entity-contact scarring in the architecture itself — structures that have been ley-exposed long enough that the damage is load-bearing. He has the category. Having it is worse than not having one.

At the edge of the geometry: movement. Something that knows these spaces better than the party. Not hostile. Not communicating. Gone before the party can close.

*ATB combat: mid-tier entity-adjacent phenomena — Shade(s), Vessel(s). First Kos glimpse — scripted, no player pursuit option.*

---

### Zone 3 — The Deep Meridian (Meridian — deep)

Wrong geometry is aggressive. Rooms have rotated. Corridors loop. Loot caches — left by people who made it this far and didn't come back. High-tier encounters.

**KOS ENCOUNTER:** The party rounds a corner and Kos is there. Not hiding — he knew they were coming. Thin, pale, carrying something he can't name. He's been in the deep geometry long enough that it doesn't bother him. For him, it's the only place in the city where something inside him is quiet.

He moves first — Bureau escapee, everyone who finds him is a threat. A brief forced fight. Iris talks through it before anyone is badly hurt: she reads him immediately (not Bureau, not enforcement, running from the same machine). He survives things he shouldn't. The entity-touch is visible — the pallor past human, the eyes tracking something nobody else can see, a quality to his stillness that isn't stillness. The party notices. Nobody says anything yet.

What follows: wariness, not warmth. He knows what's at the end of the direction. He won't say what it is — not because he's cagey, but because he doesn't have language for it. Felt knowledge, not theoretical. He gives them enough:

*"There's a place where the walls go wrong in a specific way. You'll know it. Don't touch the architecture."*

No explanation for the warning. He disappears back into the geometry before the party can press him.

Iris, in motion, one beat after he's gone — she says something to Reid. Brief. Not a speech. Something that makes clear she knows exactly what she's walking into now and chose it this time. Then she keeps moving.

*ATB combat: forced encounter with Kos — health threshold or turn count triggers Iris interruption, not a win/loss state. High-tier entity-adjacent encounters — Vessel(s), Bloom(s). Kos does not join.*

---

### Zone 4 — The Wound (Meridian — deepest point)

The geometry has stopped being random. It has a direction — the wrong space pulls like a strong current. The party can feel where they're going.

The ley architecture wound: Bureau conduit lines running through the between-space. Visible. The harvest mechanism exposed — output flowing through the lines in the open. The party can see exactly what the ley architecture does and where the output goes.

Margot pulls the document case. Her consumption spikes — the numbers that never closed — close here. This is where the output she's been tracking has been going. One or two lines. She doesn't close the case.

Karim: the source of the entity-contact scarring in every patient he's ever treated. Not the Bureau's buildings above. This.

## Case 4: Dead Signal

### Overview

After Case 3, Kurtz's response to the wound disruption tightens Bureau enforcement across the Sprawl. A Lowline resident with no Bureau file is brought to Karim's clinic — latent output activating in a body that has never expressed it, triggered by forty years of proximity to Junction 9, the Lowline's oldest ley terminal node.

The party goes to the Lowline. So does **Gideon** — Grade III Bureau enforcer, on his own initiative, doing the investigation the Bureau's blunt sweep isn't doing. He and Reid's party work the same blocks from opposite ends of the city's trust hierarchy. In Zone 2 he sees Margot. Neither speaks first.

The dungeon runs four zones through the Lowline, ending at Junction 9 substation. New enemy types introduced: Bureau Regulars (formation-dependent, contrast with Grays), Echoes (mirror practitioner output; Reid's physical attacks bypass the mechanic entirely), The Hollow (data constructs — remnant output signatures of fully-harvested practitioners running as processes in the entity's information layer; Margot reads a case number pre-combat), and The Node (boss; Phase 2 hijacks Gideon's neural interface, redirecting his healing output as self-regeneration).

Post-boss: Margot reads the terminal's output log and finds a message from someone who found Junction 9 from the Grid side. *"I have mapped forty-seven terminals exhibiting this pattern across NOX. I have the map. — S."* No location. No frequency. Open thread.

Gideon fights as a guest in Zone 4. He is cracked by what he sees. He files his report — *investigation ongoing* — and goes back to Bureau blue. He does not join.

After Case 4, the party is still Reid + Karim + Margot + Iris. Three open threads: Kos (deep Meridian), Vesper (glimpsed at the wound), Gideon (Bureau, filing open investigations), and now Soraya (47 terminals, Grid-layer map).

---

### Gideon's Investigation Entry

Gideon is in the Lowline on his own initiative — not assigned. He saw the incident report for Junction 9 (involuntary output activity, unregistered practitioners), recognized Assessment Division jurisdiction being bypassed by Kurtz's blunt Bureau sweep, and came to do the investigation correctly. He is doing real police work. He believes in the institution's stated purpose, even when the institution isn't following it.

He and Reid's party work the same blocks in Zone 2. He sees Margot. Neither speaks first. The silence is sustained through the entire dungeon.

In Zone 4 he fights alongside the party as a guest — the only option when the Node uses his Bureau-standard neural interface as a relay. Phase 2 of the Node boss fight: it hijacks his Paladin healing output as self-regeneration. The party breaks the relay. He survives.

Post-boss: one practical exchange with Margot. Neither names what they're carrying.

He files his report. Writes what he can write. *Investigation ongoing.* Puts his badge on — takes a beat longer than usual — and goes back. He does not join.

**Why he stays Bureau:** He can still write the report. After Dead Signal, his framework has a container for what he saw. The container is a stretch. He knows it is a stretch. The moment Gideon can no longer write a report that makes sense to himself is the moment he stops being Bureau. That moment is not here.

### Soraya's First Contact

First contact is indirect. After the party defeats The Node at Junction 9, Margot reads the terminal's output log and finds Soraya's notation — advanced Grid-layer analysis syntax, a format beyond Bureau standard that Margot has never encountered. The message:

This is the first one that tried to push back. If you're reading this, you found it from the physical side. I found it from the other direction. I have the map. You have the investigation. — S."*

No location. No frequency. No meeting offered.

Soraya has been monitoring Junction 9 from the Grid side for months. She knows the party found it from the physical register. She has the Grid-layer map of the entity's information architecture across NOX — forty-seven terminals, decades of mapping. She does not join. She is an open thread.
